Administrator User

The administrator user is the user who will have access to all parts of the program. They can create users and groups, view all folders of all program users, and manage and monitor activities occurring within the program.

  • User Management: They can create, modify, or delete users, as well as assign specific roles and permissions.
  • System Configuration: They have access to all settings, including customization, maintenance, and advanced configurations.
  • General Supervision: They can monitor system usage, generate comprehensive reports, and review activities performed by other users.
  • Full Access: They can view and edit any data or resource without restrictions.
  • Problem Resolution: They are capable of restoring configurations, resolving technical issues, or escalating incidents to higher levels if necessary.

    • Standard User

    The standard user is the user who will only have access to the files they own, which they can edit, delete, and manage. They cannot perform any program management tasks.

  • Access to Essential Tools: They can use the basic system functionalities assigned to them.
  • Management of Their Workspace: They can create, modify, or delete data related to their activities (e.g., documents, forms, records).
  • Restricted Access: They cannot view or modify areas reserved for administrators or users with higher permissions.

    • Group User

    This program was developed by a dedicated team of programmers passionate about technology and secure file transfer.

  • Collective Access: Shares permissions and functions assigned to the group they are part of.
  • Team Collaboration: Can access, edit, and contribute to the group's shared resources.
  • Limited Functions: Their access depends on the permissions granted to the group, which are generally more restricted than those of the administrator.
  • Management of Shared Resources: Can work on tasks related to group projects but cannot modify the group's settings or permissions.
